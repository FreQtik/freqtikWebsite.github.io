Main commercial product

Impulse Anvil.
Design the IR you need.

A focused Windows VST3 for preparing A/B sources, morphing impulse responses through time, frequency or stereo space, auditioning the result through convolution, and exporting the designed result as a reusable WAV.

01

Prepare A and B

Load recordings, samples or existing IRs, then use editing, Color and Texture controls to shape each endpoint.

02

Morph deliberately

Use time, spectral, band-swap, zig-zag, stereo, mid/side and A-to-B Lerp approaches instead of relying on one generic crossfade.

03

Bake portable WAVs

Commit the designed result as a standard WAV impulse response for compatible convolution workflows.

How the work is built

AI-assisted.
Human-directed.

AI is used intensively for implementation, research, iteration, debugging and communication. It expands what one independent developer can explore, but it does not replace responsibility. Public releases still have to work, make accurate claims, explain their limitations and remain understandable to the people using them.

The goal is not to imitate a large software company. It is to build unusually focused products with a clear personal point of view and direct accountability.
