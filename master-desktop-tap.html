What it solves

Your DAW plays fine, but the stream hears silence.

Some Windows DAW setups use ASIO and stay separate from normal desktop audio. That can make Cubase or another DAW audible in your headphones while Discord, OBS or screen share receives nothing. Master Desktop Tap is a small free beta tool made to help with exactly that problem.

1

For streaming

Use it when you want other people to hear your DAW during Discord calls, OBS recordings or screen sharing.

2

No session timer

It is free and has no built-in session-time limitation.

3

Simple purpose

It is not meant to be a creative effect. It is a routing utility for a common producer problem.

Quick setup

Put it last on your DAW master.

Start carefully, keep the level low at first and test the signal in your streaming app before going live.

01

Install

Extract the ZIP and copy the complete Master Desktop Tap.vst3 folder into your Windows VST3 folder.

02

Insert

Load Master Desktop Tap as the last insert on your DAW master channel.

03

Stream

Choose the Windows output your streaming app can hear, start with Stream Gain low, then test in Discord or OBS.
